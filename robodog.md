## What "looks like Spot" means (the visual target)
- **Upper leg (thigh / femur):** a fat, rounded, smooth tapered link — the meaty part of the leg.
- **Lower leg (tibia / shin):** a long, slim, smooth blade. In *side* view it looks near-parallel; the taper is **lateral only** (wide-flat at the ankle, slim waist at the knee, fore-aft chord ~constant).
- **Foot:** a small **flat-soled pad / teardrop** (fore-aft : lateral ≈ 3:1). NOT a ball.
- **Body:** narrow and low — copy SM3's proportions (its body already looks right). A long, low beam, not a fat box.
- **Head:** a forward-facing sensor head — the single strongest "robot dog" cue.
- **Stance:** legs upright, feet under the body (no sawhorse splay), body floating high on the legs, tibia raked slightly forward-down.

## The one hard truth (read before designing)
An SG90 is **22.7 L × 12.1 W × 22.5 H mm**, output shaft on the top face 6 mm from the gear end. Each leg joint hinges about a **lateral (Y) axis**, so the servo's 22.5 mm shaft-axis dimension points **sideways**. Therefore:

> **Every direct-drive joint must bulge to ~26 mm wide laterally to swallow a servo.** The segments *between* joints stay thin and Spot-like. That is the honest look of Spot-with-SG90: **thin bones, bulged joint housings.**

Do **not** fatten the whole leg to hide the servo — that has repeatedly produced an ugly lumpy "banana," and the servo still pokes out at the joints anyway. If a genuinely slim shin is required, the only real fix is to **relocate the knee servo up into the thigh** with a push-rod / 4-bar linkage — but that breaks "direct-drive like SM3," so only consider it if the joint bulge looks wrong after a faithful attempt.

## SG90 + hardware (locked)
- **Servo cutter** (build a parametric solid, never boolean against a servo mesh): body 22.7 × 12.1 × 22.5, tab span 32, tab thickness 2.4, output axis 6 mm from gear-end face centred on width, hub boss ø5.4 ~4 mm proud, wire exit at the motor end.
- **Coupling:** heat-set metal spline insert + M2 centre screw on each driven part (bore sized to the user's actual salvaged spline). No printed spline teeth.
- **Retention:** servo held by snap-fit + crush ribs.
- **Electronics:** Pi Pico / Pico W + PCA9685; 1S LiPo + 5 V boost.
- **Print:** ASA, enclosed printer, 0.4 mm nozzle (see **3D printing** below). Torque is marginal (~1.8 kg·cm) — keep it light; MG90S (~2.2 kg·cm) is a drop-in metal-gear upgrade, same footprint.

## 3D printing (ASA)
**Material — ASA.** Enclosed chamber, brim on every part, no draft on the door (ASA warps more than PLA — anchor layer 1). **A PLA test-fit is fine** for checking tolerances/assembly before committing ASA, but PLA is only a fit mule (won't take heat/load). Watch the shrink difference: **press/friction fits that are just-snug in PLA come out TIGHTER in ASA** — so tune the press fits (bearing cup, horn capture, femur insert) on the PLA-loose side and expect ASA a hair tighter.

**General:** 0.4 mm nozzle, 0.2 mm layers, **3–4 perimeters** (at this scale the walls carry the load, not the infill), **gyroid infill** everywhere (isotropic — no weak axis, which matters at the joint housings). ~0.15 mm elephant-foot comp on parts with first-layer features (coxa spline face, femur wire groove).

**Per-part — min gyroid infill + notes** (infill is a floor; go higher if a print feels weak):
- **Coxa** (`sm3sg90_coxa.stl`) — **35 %**. **PRINT −X FACE DOWN** (spline-hole face on the bed). **PAUSE at 4.5 mm** (top of the print-in-place horn disk-chamber, one layer before the 2 mm roof closes) and **drop the round SG90 horn in** — splined shaft pointing down into the already-printed spline hole; resume and the roof layer captures it (no glue/fastener). Bearing cup crush-ribs are a **press-fit for the 688** (they carry a +X lead-in taper so it starts square). Snap-clip slots + servo pocket print support-free in this orientation. Servo drops in from +Y after printing; cover snaps on last.
- **Coxa cover / CLIP** (`sm3sg90_coxa_cover.stl`) — **15 %** (thin/hollow — walls do the work). Print **outer face down** so the finger→leg→nub cantilever prints unsupported; don't over-squish layer 1 or the nub loses catch depth.
- **Femur insert / RING** (`sm3sg90_coxa_insert.stl`) — **50 %** (tiny friction wear surface; solid is fine). Print **seat-face down**; press-fits into the cover recess (0.1 mm). This is the *tunable* friction part — reprint in a different infill/material to change drag without touching coxa or cover.
- **Frame** — **40 %** (structural backbone / bearing-axle carrier). Print **deck-up**; the L/R shoulder girdle + gussets take the Ø8 splay pin; keep the M2 tab-screw pilots.
- **Femur** — **30 %**. Wire groove runs up the inboard print face → orient that face outward; brim + elephant-foot comp so the groove first layers stay clean. Servo2 (knee) pocket + 688/684 bearing seats.
- **Tibia / tibia_rear** — **25 %**. Slim blade — print **on its side (flat)** for strength along the length.
- **Body covers** (head / rump / trunk_lid / trunk_tub) — **12 %** (cosmetic; a couple perimeters give the cantilever latches their spring).
- **Splay pin** — **solid / 100 %** (6 walls). Tiny, must not shear.
- **Boot** — **TPU, separate plate.** 3 walls / ~20 % gyroid for grip + cushion; **print at the same scale as the foot-end** or it won't clip on.

## The joint mechanism (VERIFIED — reuse it, don't reinvent)
Built and gate-checked in `leg4.py` / `leg5.py`. Frame: **bone along X, hinge axis Y, profile in Z.**
- **Knee = rounded-knuckle direct drive.** The femur knee-end is a cylindrical cover pocket (radius RIN = R + CLR, R = 11) concentric with the Y hinge; the tibia's clevis top is a rounded cap that nests inside it. A circle rotating about its own centre sweeps itself → **the fold is collision-free by construction.** The tibia is a fork: +Y spline hub (heat-set), −Y idle peg into a femur boss, yoke joining the prongs.
- **Hip-pitch = a relocated copy of the knee** (also a Y axis): the coxa plays the femur role (holds the pitch servo), the femur proximal plays the tibia role (driven clevis).
- **Hip-roll = a −X barrel** on the coxa (cup + spline bore) mounting on the body's roll servo.
- **Travel:** knee ~110–125° (lie-down), hip-pitch ±45°, hip-roll ±45°.
- **Gates** (compute and write to a file *before* any render, so the numbers survive screenshot truncation): knee fold ≈ 0 at rest; hip-pitch interference low at neutral, ~140 at ±45°; **every part is a single solid**.

## How to make it smooth without lumps
Two viable strategies — prefer (B) if (A) keeps overshooting.

**(A) Analytic-ellipse lofts.** Build rings by scaling a `Part.Circle` to an ellipse (`transformGeometry`) — never loft faceted `makePolygon` rings (those give corncob stripes). Loft with `Part.makeLoft(wires, True, False)` (B-spline, ruled=False) and set `obj.ViewObject.Deviation = 0.02`.
- **Trap:** B-spline lofts *overshoot* when the section size ramps along the length — a 9→14 ramp ballooned to ~24 and broke the knee gate. **Keep each cross-section parameter constant or only very gently varying.** Constant section = exact width, no overshoot.
- **Gotcha (this session):** scaling a `Part.Circle` with `transformGeometry` scales about the WORLD origin, so a ring built at `(cx,0,cz)` drifts to centre `(cx, 0, cz·hz)` and the femur explodes in Z (saw 195 mm). Build the circle **at the origin**, scale, *then* `translate` it to its X station. See `dog.py:_ellA`.
- **Counter-intuitive but verified:** a *beefier* mid-bulge (ends frozen narrow, cosine bump to ~16 mm lat / ~14.5 vert) actually CLEARED the hip-pitch +45° gate the slim femur was failing (15943→0 mm³) — the B-spline reshapes the proximal end. So beef the thigh for the SM3 look; it won't break the gate as long as the joint ends stay frozen.

**(B) Primitive shells (more predictable).** Build the cosmetic links from rounded boxes / capsules / tapered prisms fused together. These never balloon, slice cleanly, and real Spot links are fairly geometric anyway. Often the more reliable path to a clean look.

Either way: **grow the cosmetic skin over the verified joint cores, then re-cut the servo pocket last** (so the pocket stays open). Don't redrape the joints. `makeFillet` throws `StdFail_NotDone` on lofted solids — bake roundness into the sections/primitives, don't fillet afterward.
